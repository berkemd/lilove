# Privacy Policy

**Effective Date**: October 28, 2025  
**Last Updated**: October 28, 2025

## Introduction

LiLove ("we", "our", or "us") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal data. This privacy policy explains how we collect, use, store, and share your information when you use our web application and mobile app (collectively, the "Service").

## Data We Collect

### Information You Provide

**Account Information:**
- Email address (required for account creation)
- Username/display name
- Password (stored encrypted with bcrypt)
- Profile picture (optional)
- Social media links (optional: Twitter, LinkedIn, GitHub, Instagram)

**Profile & Preferences:**
- Language preference
- Timezone
- Notification settings
- Privacy settings (public/private profile)

**Goals & Tasks:**
- Goals you create (title, description, category, priority, deadline)
- Tasks and subtasks
- Habit tracking data
- Progress and completion records
- Time tracking data (Pomodoro sessions)

**AI Coaching Data:**
- Messages sent to AI coach
- AI responses and recommendations
- Action plans generated
- Coach interaction history

**Team & Social Data:**
- Team memberships
- Challenges you participate in
- Friend connections
- Comments and interactions

**Payment Information:**
- Billing name and address (processed by Paddle)
- Subscription plan and status
- Payment history and invoices
- We **do not** store credit card numbers (handled by payment processors)

### Information We Collect Automatically

**Usage Data:**
- Pages visited and features used
- Time spent in the app
- Click patterns and navigation
- Device type and browser information
- IP address and approximate location (city/country level)

**Performance Data:**
- App errors and crash reports
- API response times
- Page load performance

**Analytics:**
- Product usage analytics via PostHog
- Feature adoption metrics
- User engagement patterns

### Information from Third Parties

**OAuth Providers:**
- When you sign in with Google or Apple, we receive:
  - Email address
  - Name
  - Profile picture
  - Provider account ID

## How We Use Your Data

### Primary Uses

1. **Provide the Service**
   - Create and manage your account
   - Store your goals, tasks, and habits
   - Provide AI coaching based on your data
   - Enable team collaboration features
   - Process payments and manage subscriptions

2. **Improve the Service**
   - Analyze usage patterns to improve features
   - Fix bugs and performance issues
   - Develop new features based on user needs
   - Conduct A/B testing for UX improvements

3. **Communications**
   - Send transactional emails (password resets, payment confirmations)
   - Send notification emails (goal reminders, team invites) if you opt in
   - Send product updates and announcements (you can opt out)

4. **Security & Fraud Prevention**
   - Detect and prevent unauthorized access
   - Identify and block malicious activity
   - Investigate security incidents
   - Comply with legal obligations

### Legal Bases for Processing (GDPR)

- **Contract Performance**: Processing necessary to provide the Service
- **Legitimate Interests**: Improving the Service, fraud prevention, security
- **Consent**: Marketing communications, optional features
- **Legal Obligation**: Complying with tax, accounting, and legal requirements

## How We Share Your Data

### We Share Data With:

**Service Providers:**
- **Neon (PostgreSQL)**: Database hosting (data encrypted at rest)
- **Replit**: Web hosting and deployment
- **OpenAI**: AI coaching (messages sent to GPT-4 API)
- **Paddle**: Payment processing (billing and subscription management)
- **Apple**: iOS in-app purchases and app distribution
- **PostHog**: Product analytics (anonymized data)
- **Sentry** (optional): Error tracking (no PII in error reports)

**Legal Requirements:**
We may disclose your data if required by law, court order, or government request.

**Business Transfers:**
If we merge, sell, or transfer assets, your data may be transferred to the new owner.

### We Do NOT:

- ❌ Sell your personal data to third parties
- ❌ Share your data with advertisers
- ❌ Use your data for targeted advertising
- ❌ Share your goals or personal information publicly without your consent

## Your Privacy Choices

### Account Management

**Access Your Data**: Request a copy of all your data (JSON export)  
**Update Your Data**: Edit your profile, goals, and settings anytime  
**Delete Your Account**: Permanently delete your account and all data  
**Export Your Data**: Download all your data in portable format (GDPR right to portability)

### Privacy Settings

**Profile Visibility**: Choose public or private profile  
**Search Visibility**: Control whether you appear in user search  
**Activity Visibility**: Control what activities are visible to teams/friends

### Communication Preferences

**Email Notifications**: Opt in/out of goal reminders, team invites, achievements  
**Push Notifications** (iOS): Manage in device settings  
**Marketing Emails**: Unsubscribe anytime via link in email footer

### Cookies & Tracking

**Essential Cookies**: Required for authentication and session management (cannot be disabled)  
**Analytics Cookies**: PostHog analytics (you can opt out in settings)  
**No Advertising Cookies**: We don't use advertising or tracking pixels

## Data Retention

**Active Accounts**: We retain your data as long as your account is active  
**Deleted Accounts**: Data deleted within 30 days of account deletion (backups may persist up to 90 days)  
**Legal Holds**: Data may be retained longer if required by law

**Specific Retention Periods:**
- Session logs: 7 days
- Error logs: 90 days
- Payment records: 7 years (tax compliance)
- Support tickets: 3 years

## Data Security

### Technical Measures

- **Encryption in Transit**: HTTPS with TLS 1.2+ for all connections
- **Encryption at Rest**: Database encrypted by Neon PostgreSQL
- **Password Security**: bcrypt hashing with 10 rounds
- **Session Security**: httpOnly, secure, sameSite=strict cookies
- **Access Controls**: Role-based access control (RBAC) for internal systems
- **Vulnerability Scanning**: Automated CodeQL and dependency audits

### Organizational Measures

- **Employee Access**: Minimal access on need-to-know basis
- **Background Checks**: All employees undergo background checks
- **Security Training**: Regular security awareness training
- **Incident Response**: 24-hour incident response plan

## International Data Transfers

**Data Location**: Your data is primarily stored in the United States (Neon data centers)

**EU Users**: Data may be transferred outside the EU. We ensure adequate protection through:
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) with service providers
- Service providers with GDPR compliance certifications

## Children's Privacy

LiLove is **not intended for children under 13 years old**. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. If we discover we've collected data from a child under 13, we'll delete it immediately.

**Parents**: If you believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at privacy@lilove.org.

## Your Rights (GDPR/CCPA)

### GDPR Rights (EU Residents)

1. **Right to Access**: Request a copy of your personal data
2. **Right to Rectification**: Correct inaccurate data
3. **Right to Erasure**: Delete your data ("right to be forgotten")
4. **Right to Restriction**: Limit how we process your data
5. **Right to Portability**: Export your data in machine-readable format
6. **Right to Object**: Object to processing based on legitimate interests
7. **Right to Withdraw Consent**: Withdraw consent for optional features
8. **Right to Lodge a Complaint**: File complaint with supervisory authority

### CCPA Rights (California Residents)

1. **Right to Know**: Request disclosure of data we collect
2. **Right to Delete**: Request deletion of your data
3. **Right to Opt-Out**: Opt out of data "sales" (we don't sell data)
4. **Right to Non-Discrimination**: We won't discriminate for exercising your rights

### How to Exercise Your Rights

Email us at: **privacy@lilove.org**  
Or use in-app tools: Settings → Privacy → Data Rights

We'll respond within 30 days (GDPR) or 45 days (CCPA).
